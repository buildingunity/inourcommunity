The Youth Mental
Health Ambassadors

The Youth Volunteer Corps Mental Health Ambassador Program was developed in partnership with the nonprofit, NAMI (National Alliance on Mental Illness) for middle and high school students to educate participants on youth mental health issues and supporting the mental health of their peers. In this program, student participants go through a series of trainings to learn more about mental health, resources for support, and suicide prevention efforts. Upon completion of the trainings, VOiCEup Berks staff work with Ambassadors to develop projects that support student mental health, raise awareness, reduce stigmas, educate others, and/or work with community organizations and other Ambassadors on suicide prevention efforts.

Muhlenberg
Aevidum Club

Aevidum is a 501(c)3 non-profit organization that empowers youth to shatter the silence surrounding depression, suicide, and other issues facing teens. The word Aevidum, which means “I’ve got your back,” was created by students after a classmate died by suicide. Aevidum inspires schools and communities to adopt cultures of care and advocacy, encouraging all members to have their friends’ backs.

Organizing this event from Aevidum: Lauren Heydt, Ashley De Los Santos, Isabella Turner, Quinn Wagner, Sharmara Callender, & Alyssa Hoffman
